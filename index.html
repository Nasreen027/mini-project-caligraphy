Calligraphy

Calligraphy is the kind of music not for the ears but for the eyes

Art of beautiful, stylized, or elegant handwriting or lettering with pen or brush and ink. It involves the correct formation of characters, the ordering of the various parts, and the harmony of proportions. In the Islamic and Chinese cultures, calligraphy is as highly revered as painting.

Frequently asked questions

Have questions? we're here to help you.
  • What was the main pupose of calligraphy as an art?

  • Calligraphy aims to produce a reaction, just like any quality piece of sculpture or painting, its goals are to invoke a deeper meaning and communicate with the viewer on both the linguistic and creative level.

  • How does calligraphy influence the world today?

  • The Arabic calligraphy had a great impact in the improvement of arts all over the world, as it has influenced cultures such as Andalusia in Spain and more, plus it has been the main influence in multiple art styles such as the Baroque and Rococo art styles in Europe, and also the fusions resulted from many cultural ...

  • Who first started calligraphy?

  • The origin of Calligraphy with brushes dates back to ancient China during the Shang dynasty becoming more common during the Han dynasty (206 BCE – 220 CE) where it was expected for all educated men and some women to be proficient at it.

  • What are the three main types of caligraphy?

  • There are three main types of calligraphy: western, eastern, and Arabic. Each type reflects the language and handwriting of a different region of the world.

  • Why is it called calligraphy?

  • calligraphy, the art of beautiful handwriting. The term may derive from the Greek words for “beauty” (kallos) and “to write” (graphein).

  • Why are people interested in calligraphy?

  • Besides the mental benefits, it's satisfying to create something beautiful and tangible, and there's also something captivating about watching people create videos of their calligraphy. Plus, calligraphy is difficult.

Get to know us

Qalam came into being when three students who happened to be aesthetes came together and realised there is endless talent and passion in Pakistan for art but little support for it. From here they embarked on a difficult but rewarding journey to create a platform that would bridge this gap and bring artists and art lovers. Within just a few months of its existence, Qalam has now over 100 artists onboard from all across the country specializing in acrylics, oils, pastels, calligraphy, landscapes, pop art, digital illustrations, basically every form of art you can think of. Qalam is Pakistan’s first online platform that is built for art lovers and artists.Qalam is not a buying and selling site for art but it is an initiative to promote art in Pakistan and make it easily accessible and available to the masses. Our vision is to create a space where local artists find support and encouragement to explore their creativity and talent, and showcase their art to appreciative individuals.
